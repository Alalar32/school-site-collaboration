Welcome to the Robotics Department

Discover innovation through creativity, coding, and engineering.

About the Robotics

Robotics is an exciting field that blends mechanical design, electronics, and programming to create intelligent machines capable of performing tasks automatically.

This program introduces students to the fundamentals of robotics and automation, helping them understand how machines think, move, and interact with their environment.

Students will explore hands-on projects, learn how to program microcontrollers, and develop problem-solving skills that prepare them for careers in technology, engineering, and innovation.

Curriculum

Introduction to Robotics and Automation

Learn what robotics is, how robots are used in different industries, and how automation is shaping the future.

Components of a Robot (Sensors, Motors, and Controllers)

Understand the key parts that make robots function, including sensors for input, motors for movement, and controllers for processing commands.

Electronics and Circuit Design

Explore how circuits power robots, how to connect components safely, and how to use breadboards and microcontrollers.

Programming with Arduino / Raspberry Pi

Discover how to write code that brings your robots to life using platforms like Arduino or Raspberry Pi.

Robot Control and Movement

Learn how to control motors, wheels, and actuators to make your robot move and respond to its environment.

Building Line-Following and Obstacle-Avoidance Robots

Apply your skills to build robots that follow paths or avoid obstacles using sensors and logic-based programming.

Wireless and Bluetooth-Controlled Robots

Create robots that can be remotely controlled using wireless technology or Bluetooth connectivity.

Introduction to Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in Robotics

Explore how AI allows robots to learn, adapt, and make decisions on their own.

Program Information

  • Duration: 8 Weeks
  • Level: Beginner to Intermediate
  • Mode: On-campus / Online
  • Requirements: No prior programming experience needed. Basic computer knowledge is enough.
